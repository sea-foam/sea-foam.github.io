our philosophy:

  • Emphasis

    if you're making a toaster, emphasize its ability to make toast, and de-emphasize everything else.
    it's better to be a great toaster than it is to be a bad camera that makes mediocre toast.

  • Simplicity

    everyone knows how to make toast. insert bread, push the plunger down.
    don't make a bluetooth connected toaster that can only be activated via companion app. if my phone breaks, or the app gets a bad update, or something happens inside the toaster, not only can I not make toast anymore, but I have to diagnose which of the 3 is causing the issue before I can get to fixing it.

  • Parts

    parts should be replaceable, and things should be made to be repaired.
    if a part of a thing breaks, it should be easier to replace that part than it is to replace the thing.
    if your thing is made of many small, easy to make parts, and one breaks, that is still easier to fix than one large, hard to make part.
    most importantly: make things in an easily understood way. if you wake up to a broken toaster, you want to understand why it is broken and how to fix it within minutes rather than days.

  • Place

    things have a place.
    a toaster is a great thing, but no matter how well it does its job it can't do everything. you could try to make breakfast using just a toaster, but it probably wouldn't go well. however, if you combine many things, like your toaster, a stove, a refrigerator, some pans, and dishes (which are also all made to do their job well), you'll have made an incredible breakfast (which is also a new thing)!

  • Beauty

    make beautiful things.
    make things that are wonderful, make things that are simple, make things that you are proud of. make things that will change the world. make things that will never see the light of day.
    just make things, and if you follow the advice above, your thing will be beautiful.
